Clarinet

The clarinet is a member of the woodwind group which in addition to strings, percussion, and brass make up most of the common instruments used today. It was invented in the late 1700s, making it one of the last common instruments to become mainstream.

It is featured in many different settings such as in an orchestra, wind band, a solo instrument in jazz and klezmer (Jewish music), movie scores, and Dixieland.

Usually made of African grenadilla wood, the clarinet is known for its dark, smooth tone. Usually pitched the in key of Bb, it has many auxiliary instruments such as the bass clarinet pitched an octave below and the piccolo clarinet usually pitch in Eb above.
